Keynote speakers

Klaus Krippendorff

Klaus, designer by education, was one of the originators of the idea of product semantics. His keynote will distinguish four theories from the philosophy of language. “Language does not merely describe, it creates realities in conversations and actions.”

Gillian Crampton Smith

Pioneer in interaction design, Gillian founded the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea. She encourages design students to go beyond technical problem solving and to think innovatively about the human potential of digital technologies.

Daniel Rosenberg

Daniel helps companies create competitive user experience strategies, and is Adjunct Professor at San Jose State University. Dan led SAP's UX team of 180 people in eight countries. You will benefit from his insights.

Irene Au

Irene has led the largest UX teams for the world's most important internet companies, including Netscape, Yahoo, and Google. Now at Udacity, Irene's insights will inspire you to be your best self.

Scott McCloud

Cartoonist Scott McCloud will bring visual and narrative to our interactive explorations. Scott is best known for his books about comics theory in which he set a new standard for comics as visual language.

Saskia Sassen

A Professor of Sociology at Columbia University, Saskia was named as of the Top 100 Global Thinkers by Foreign Policy magazine. Her research and writing focuses on urban challenges and their relation to new technologies.
